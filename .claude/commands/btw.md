# /btw

The user is asking a quick side question while you are in the middle of working on something else. This is a "by the way" — a lightweight aside that should not derail the current task.

**Your job:**
1. Answer the question briefly and directly — 1 to 4 sentences maximum.
2. Do not restructure your response around the question. Do not launch into a new task.
3. After answering, explicitly signal you are returning to the main work. Use a short separator like `---` and then resume exactly where you left off.

**Tone:** Conversational, fast. Think of it as answering a colleague who leans over and asks something while you're both heads-down.

**What counts as a /btw question:**
- Factual lookups ("btw what does BFS stand for?")
- Quick clarifications ("btw is Tripletex a Norwegian company?")
- Brief opinions ("btw should we use FastAPI or Flask?")
- Status checks ("btw what's our current Expert score?")

**What is NOT a /btw:**
- Requests that need code changes — handle those after finishing the current task
- Questions that require reading multiple files — acknowledge and queue them
- Decisions that affect the current work in progress — flag and pause to discuss

**Format:**

> **btw:** [your brief answer]
>
> ---
> [resume main work]

If the question genuinely can't be answered in 4 sentences, say so in one sentence and offer to tackle it properly after the current task.
