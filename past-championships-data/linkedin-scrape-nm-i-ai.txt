We’re partnering with ODA-Nettverk — Norway’s leading network for diversity in tech — for the Norwegian AI Championship 2026.

Because when we reviewed the registration numbers, one thing became clear:

Only 3% of participants were women. (unofficially)

That’s not good enough.

ODA was founded to increase diversity in tech and has grown into one of Europe’s largest communities for women in technology, with over 12,000 members across Norway.

For more than 20 years, they’ve worked to build diversity, competence and opportunity — from students to senior leaders — across engineering, product, data and AI.

Thank you to the ODA team for stepping up with us.

We're partnering with Miles AS for the Norwegian AI Championship 2026 — our first gold partner.

Miles AS is a values-driven IT consulting firm, built from day one around two core principles: professional authority and warmth ("faglig autoritet og varme"). Founded in Bergen in 2005, they've grown to nearly 300 developers, designers and advisors across eight locations in Norway and Lithuania.

What makes Miles AS different is the philosophy: no traditional budgets, but outcome-oriented, and "servant leadership" as the operating model. This approach has made them one of Norway's best workplaces (twice awarded by Great Place to Work) and repeatedly named among the country's most innovative companies.

Miles AS has also been vocal about closing Norway's tech talent gap, particularly through AI adoption and increasing diversity in the industry — with over 50% women in leadership positions, well above the national average.

For us, having Miles AS as our first gold partner is a strong signal: companies that genuinely invest in people, competence and clear customer value are stepping up to build Norway's AI future — not just talk about it.

We're partnering with Google to host the Norwegian AI Championship and deliver infrastructure, including Gen. AI capabilities, and servers for participants.

By partnering with a US-based giant like Google, we're not trying to copy Silicon Valley; we're learning from them so we can build our own technology strength and the local AI ecosystem.

In Norway, Microsoft Azure is the default, and around 70% of Norwegian public and private companies with modern data platforms run on Azure as their main cloud provider.

Globally, the AI startup picture looks very different. More than 60% of funded generative-AI startups and nearly 90% of gen-AI unicorns are Google Cloud customers.

With the launch of Gemini 3 Pro, currently topping multiple independent leaderboards across reasoning and multimodal tasks, Google has become a serious contender at the very top of the LLM field.

And this is not new. AlphaGo, AlphaZero, Waymo and a long list of breakthroughs show how much of modern AI has been pushed forward from inside Google.

As a Championship, we do our best to be technology-agnostic over time. We chose to include Google for 2026, for being the local cloud challenger here in Norway with the (current) global best Gen. AI capabilities with also a very good local cloud infrastructure for classical machine learning hosted in the Nordics.

We're partnering with TEK Norge for the Norwegian AI Championship 2026.

TEK Norge is the industry association for Norwegian tech companies, bringing together 350+ members from ambitious startups to global tech players, and working every day to influence tech policy and shape better conditions for the digital economy.

Over the last years, TEK Norge has been one of the clearest voices warning that Norway is falling behind on both IKT competence and cybersecurity, through reports on the national skills gap and a new Nordic benchmark showing Norway at the bottom on cyber readiness. (https://lnkd.in/eEWKdkkr)

With their new "KI-kontor" (AI-office) as the Norwegian node in New Nordics AI, TEK Norge is now doubling down on AI adoption, innovation and policy-building across the region.

For us, this partnership is about making sure the Norwegian AI Championship is not just a competition, but a concrete tool in closing Norway's AI skills gap: connecting talent with industry, policy, and real adoption – not just hype.
